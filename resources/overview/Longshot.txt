
  <h2>Overview</h2>
  <p><strong>Dick Francis</strong> was a renowned British author celebrated for his thrilling novels set in the world of horse racing. In 1996, he was named <em>Grand Master</em> by the Mystery Writers of America for a lifetime's achievement. Two years later, in 1998, he was elected a <em>fellow of the Royal Society of Literature</em>, and in 2000, he was awarded a <em>Commander of the Order of the British Empire (CBE)</em> in the Queen's Birthday Honours List.</p>
  <p>Francis passed away in February 2010 at the age of eighty-nine. Despite his passing, he remains one of the greatest thriller writers of all time, with a literary legacy that continues to captivate readers worldwide.</p>
